Random-circuit sampling (RCS) produces output probabilities whose chaotic-limit statistics are well approximated by the PorterThomas (PT) law. This extended work addresses ve key limitations of preliminary studies and presents a comprehensive framework
for transforming PT-like samples into three trainingtime distributions for embodied learning: (i) a samplescheduling distribution over long-tail buckets, (ii) a riskscene distribution over perturbation severities, and (iii)
an exploration-noise distribution over latent action perturbations. The extensions include: (1) real-world validation on Meta-World MT10/MT50 benchmarks; (2)
multidimensional optimal transport via Copula preservation; (3) nonlinear utility functions and adaptive
scheduling; (4) integration with real quantum hardware
data; (5) comprehensive baseline comparison. On MetaWorld MT10, PT-rank improves tail success from 52.9%
(uniform) to 56.5% with 94.3% retention on MT50.
Real quantum hardware experiments demonstrate robustness with only 3.2% degradation. These results establish that PT-style quantum randomness admits implementable, theoretically grounded mappings into longtail-aware training distributions.

Preliminaries

2.1

PorterThomas Surrogate

Let U be an n-qubit pseudo-random circuit with output
probability pU (x) = |⟨x|U |0n ⟩|2 . The rescaled variable
Yx = N · pU (x) approaches Exp(1) in the chaotic limit.
PT-like source samples are modeled by Y ∼ µPT with
FPT (y) = 1 − e−y .

Embodied learning systems are trained under distributions that are rarely uniform across task families, object categories, contact modes, failure modes, and disturbance regimes. Even when the policy class is expressive enough, performance may remain fragile because the
learner receives too little exposure to the regions that
dominate tail risk. The central object of design is therefore not only the policy but also the training distribution
itself.
Random-circuit sampling provides a technically attractive source distribution for this problem. For sufciently deep pseudo-random circuits, the output probabilities exhibit anti-concentration and are well modeled
by PorterThomas statistics [2, 1]. PT samples do not
carry task semantics; their value lies in the structured,

Conclusion

A comprehensive framework has been presented
for transforming PT-style quantum randomness into
training-time distributions for long-tail embodied learning. Sample scheduling is solved by permutation-optimal
rank matching; risk and exploration mappings are solved
by monotone quantile transport; the framework extends
to multidimensional perturbations via Copula preservation. Validation on Meta-World MT10/MT50, integration of real quantum hardware data, and comprehensive baseline comparison establish that PT-style quantum randomness admits implementable, theoretically
grounded mappings with validated real-world applicability.
